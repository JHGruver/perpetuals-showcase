How It Works

Kronos X is a distributed matching engine and complete exchange platform. It handles everything from order intake to execution to settlement. Every piece of the Perpetuals exchange — from matching engine to settlement, custody to compliance — runs on this single platform.

API Gateway
Entry point for all trading operations. Handles REST and WebSocket APIs, authentication, rate limiting, and request routing. Provides real-time market data streaming and order management.
Matching Engine
Core order matching with price-time priority. Processes 1–2 million orders per second with sub-millisecond latency. Distributed architecture ensures no single point of failure.
Risk Engine
Real-time pre-trade and post-trade risk calculations. Manages margin requirements, position limits, and portfolio risk. Integrates directly with BayesShield AI for intelligent risk scoring.
BayesShield (Embedded)
AI-powered market surveillance module embedded directly in the exchange stack. Monitors for manipulation patterns, wash trading, and anomalous order flow in real-time.
Settlement Layer
Trade settlement and clearing powered by Ledgera. Achieves T+0 (instant) settlement across multiple blockchain networks. No third-party CCP dependency.
Vault
Integrated custody solution with tiered storage architecture. Post-quantum cryptography, multi-signature authorization, and HSM-backed key management.

Why It Matters

The matching engine is the heart of any exchange. Kronos X being built in-house means Perpetuals has zero dependency on third-party exchange infrastructure. This enables self-clearing, custom product types like Barrier Futures, and complete control over the trading experience.

Barrier Futures

No-liquidation derivatives that solve the $150B annual forced liquidation problem

$150BAnnual forced liquidations (the problem)
0Margin calls
0Funding fees
1stRegulated product of its kind under MTF

The Problem

Traditional perpetual swaps have cascading liquidations: one position is liquidated, pushing the price further, which liquidates the next position, creating a chain reaction. In 2025 alone, $150 billion in forced liquidations occurred across crypto derivatives markets. ESMA data shows 74–89% of retail CFD/derivative accounts lose money.

How Barrier Futures Work

A Barrier Future closes automatically at a pre-set barrier price — the user's chosen risk limit. There are no margin calls, no funding fees, and no liquidation cascades. The barrier IS the risk limit. Users set it at entry and know their maximum possible loss upfront. Same leveraged exposure as a perpetual swap, fundamentally different risk profile.

For Brokers
A product they can offer clients without the reputation risk of liquidation cascades. Clean regulatory story under MiFID II. No need to explain why a client lost more than they deposited.
For Retail Traders
Leveraged exposure without the nightmare scenario. Maximum loss is known upfront. No midnight margin calls. Position simply closes at the barrier price.
For Regulators
A derivatives product that actually protects retail users. Directly addresses ESMA's concerns about retail CFD losses. First regulated product of its kind under an MTF license.
UX Design Opportunity
The barrier slider with real-time P&L simulation is the key interface. "If BTC drops to this price, your position closes. Your maximum loss is $X." Progressive disclosure of complexity — simple for beginners, full depth for professionals.

Why It Matters

Barrier Futures is a genuinely novel financial product. No one else has this under an MTF license. It's the primary reason Perpetuals exists — the product that justifies the entire regulatory and infrastructure investment.

How It Works

BayesShield surfaces a risk score, not a verdict. It's a gradient risk signal, not a binary warning. For any trade, it can provide historical context: "Trades with similar parameters had a 73% loss rate in the last 30 days." The signal is opt-in depth — users see the score and can tap for the reasoning.

BayesShield does not block trades. It creates friction, not prohibition. The framing is: "This risk intelligence is part of Perpetuals' commitment to investor protection under MiFID II."

Market Surveillance
Real-time monitoring for manipulation patterns, wash trading, spoofing, and anomalous order flow. Embedded directly in the Kronos X stack for zero-latency detection.
Risk Scoring
Each trade receives a risk assessment based on 11.7 billion historical trades. Scores are presented as a spectrum, not a binary pass/fail. Users can drill into the reasoning behind each score.
Institutional Use
For brokers and institutional clients, BayesShield serves as a compliance tool — demonstrating regulatory commitment to investor protection and market integrity.
Retail Use
For retail traders, BayesShield acts as an intelligence tool — providing an informational edge that no other platform offers. A premium feature, not a restriction.

Why It Matters

No other crypto exchange has an AI risk engine at this scale. BayesShield is a massive competitive differentiator. The design challenge is making it feel like a premium feature, not a nanny.

How It Works

Perpetuals Vault is a fully self-custodied solution with zero third-party dependency. Assets are stored across a tiered architecture designed to balance liquidity needs with maximum security.

Hot Wallet (15%)
Liquidity layer for immediate trading needs. Connected to the Kronos X matching engine for instant order execution. Protected by real-time monitoring and rate limiting.
Warm Storage (35%)
Intermediate storage tier for assets that need to be mobilized within hours. Multi-signature authorization required for transfers. HSM-backed key management.
Cold Vault (50%)
Maximum-security offline storage for the majority of assets. Hardware-secured with post-quantum cryptography. Requires multiple physical authorizations to access.

Why It Matters

After FTX, custody is the #1 concern for institutional clients. Perpetuals Vault being self-custodied with no third-party dependency directly addresses the trust deficit that defines post-FTX crypto.

How It Works

Ledgera is a meta-blockchain settlement layer that provides instant (T+0) cross-chain settlement. It supports multiple chains — ETH, BTC, USDC, and others — with real-time clearing and finality. No intermediary clearinghouse required.

The EU T+1 settlement mandate doesn't take effect until October 2027. Ledgera already operates at T+0, exceeding the requirement by over two years. This is a significant competitive and regulatory advantage.

Tokenized Securities Settlement
Ledgera provides the infrastructure for tokenized securities settlement — the same capability Gruhn pioneered at FTX Europe with the world's first regulatory-approved tokenized stocks.
Consumer Brand: Ledgera Console
Ledgera also exists as a standalone consumer brand within the Perpetuals ecosystem — the "Ledgera Console" for digital asset settlement services. Secure, compliant settlement infrastructure for tokenized securities.

Why It Matters

Settlement speed is becoming a regulatory differentiator. When the EU T+1 mandate hits in October 2027, exchanges that can't meet it will be forced out. Ledgera's T+0 capability means Perpetuals is already years ahead.

Core Product

PerpetualPay

Fiat on/off ramp connecting traditional banking to digital assets

How It Works

PerpetualPay is the payments layer that bridges traditional banking infrastructure and digital assets. It enables users to move money between conventional bank accounts and the Perpetuals platform — the "last mile" that connects old money to new infrastructure.

Without a fiat on/off ramp, the exchange is only accessible to users who already hold crypto. PerpetualPay removes that barrier, making the platform accessible to traditional institutional clients and first-time crypto users alike.

Why It Matters

The fiat bridge is what makes Perpetuals accessible to institutional clients who manage assets in traditional currencies. It's the entry point for the "institutional shift" — traditional finance capital flowing into crypto derivatives.

Glossary

MTF
Multilateral Trading Facility. A regulated trading venue under MiFID II. Same classification as traditional stock exchanges.
MiFID II
Markets in Financial Instruments Directive II. EU regulation governing investment services. Enables cross-border passporting.
MiCA
Markets in Crypto-Assets. Comprehensive EU crypto regulation. Full enforcement July 1, 2026.
DORA
Digital Operational Resilience Act. EU regulation requiring ICT risk management and resilience testing for financial entities.
EMIR
European Market Infrastructure Regulation. Rules for OTC derivatives, central counterparties, and trade repositories.
CySEC
Cyprus Securities and Exchange Commission. The regulatory authority that issued Perpetuals' MTF license.
T+0 / T+1
Settlement timing. T+0 = instant settlement. T+1 = settlement one day after trade. EU mandates T+1 by Oct 2027.
GLS
Grid Ledger System. Kobayashi's proprietary hybrid blockchain with 0.016s transaction speed.
HSM
Hardware Security Module. Physical device for managing cryptographic keys. Used in Perpetuals Vault.
KYC/AML
Know Your Customer / Anti-Money Laundering. Identity verification and transaction monitoring requirements.
Perpetual Futures
Derivative contracts with no expiry date. Traders can hold positions indefinitely, paying periodic funding rates.
Barrier Futures
Perpetuals' novel derivative. Position auto-closes at a pre-set barrier price. No margin calls, no funding fees, no cascading liquidations.
Funding Rate
Periodic payment between long and short positions in perpetual futures. Keeps the contract price anchored to spot. Barrier Futures eliminate this.
Liquidation
Forced closure of a leveraged position when margin is insufficient. Cascading liquidations are the $150B problem Barrier Futures solve.
Open Interest
Total value of outstanding derivative contracts. Peak crypto OI reached $235.9B in October 2025.
Self-clearing
Ability to settle trades without a third-party clearinghouse (CCP). Enabled by Kronos X + GLS integration.
Passporting
Using a license from one EU/EEA country to operate across all 30 member states without additional licenses.
ESMA
European Securities and Markets Authority. EU-level regulatory body. Issues guidance on CFD restrictions and investor protection.
